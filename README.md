# Optimizing Inventory and Sales Forecasting at Pastry Palace

### [cite_start]A Data-Driven Approach to Efficiency and Organization [cite: 1]

## 📌 Project Overview
[cite_start]This project focuses on transforming the operational model of **Pastry Palace**, a local snacks and bakery shop in Dakra, Jharkhand[cite: 16, 17]. [cite_start]Historically, the business relied on the owner's intuition for all decision-making[cite: 24]. [cite_start]This study implements a data-driven framework to categorize products by profitability, optimize inventory, and reduce financial losses caused by the wastage of perishable items[cite: 26, 31, 33].

## 📉 Key Business Challenges
1. [cite_start]**Lack of Recorded Data:** Inability to categorize products by profit contribution due to no formal record-keeping[cite: 27, 28].
2. [cite_start]**Operational Waste:** Frequent wastage of "Fresh" perishable items due to a lack of a daily sales or stock tracking system[cite: 32, 33].
3. [cite_start]**Demand Forecasting:** Difficulty in predicting sales patterns for a diverse product mix ranging from daily essentials to event-driven items[cite: 23, 34].

## 🛠️ Methodology & Data Collection
* [cite_start]**Data Source:** Primary data generated from scratch through owner interviews and on-site observations[cite: 38, 40].
* [cite_start]**Scope:** A 50-day period (June 1 – July 20, 2025) covering 36 unique products[cite: 41].
* **Analytical Frameworks:**
    * [cite_start]**ABC Analysis:** Ranking products by total profit contribution[cite: 49].
    * [cite_start]**CV (Coefficient of Variation) Analysis:** Measuring sales volatility to identify predictable vs. erratic demand[cite: 51].
    * [cite_start]**Wastage Analysis:** Evaluating both unit-volume loss and financial cost of unsold items[cite: 53].
    * [cite_start]**Product Portfolio Matrix:** Categorizing items into Stars, Opportunities, Workhorses, and Dogs[cite: 55, 140].

## 🚀 Key Insights & Results
* [cite_start]**The 80/20 Rule:** 47% of the product variety (top 16 items) generates over 80% of the total profit[cite: 63].
* [cite_start]**Profit Drivers:** "Fresh" items are the primary profit drivers, with **Chocolate and Butterscotch Cakes** alone contributing nearly 23% of total profit[cite: 64, 65].
* **Sales Predictability:**
    * [cite_start]**Stable:** Momos, Sandwiches, and Pav Bhaji (CV < 30%)[cite: 92, 93].
    * [cite_start]**Volatile:** Birthday items (Poppers, Caps) and specialty goods (CV > 50%)[cite: 91].
* [cite_start]**Hidden Losses:** While low-cost snacks are wasted more frequently, wasting a single high-cost item like a cake (₹400+ loss) is more financially damaging than many small items combined[cite: 98, 99].

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
* [cite_start]**Target "Opportunities":** Shift marketing focus to high-profit but lower-volume items like Pizzas and specialty Pastries[cite: 139, 143].
* [cite_start]**Differentiated Inventory:** Implement a "safety stock" approach for volatile birthday items and data-driven daily targets for stable sellers like Momos[cite: 144].
* [cite_start]**Waste Mitigation:** Encourage pre-orders for expensive cakes to minimize high-cost waste[cite: 145].
