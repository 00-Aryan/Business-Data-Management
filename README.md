# Optimizing Inventory and Sales Forecasting at Pastry Palace
### A Data-Driven Approach to Efficiency and Organization

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on the digital transformation of **Pastry Palace** (Dakra, Jharkhand). By shifting from intuition-based management to a data-driven model, I analyzed 50 days of sales to identify key profit drivers and reduce the financial impact of perishability.

## 📉 Business Challenges
* **Inventory Inefficiency:** Lack of a formal system led to frequent wastage of fresh items.
* **Profit Strategy:** Difficulty in identifying which products to promote or remove due to missing records.
* **High-Cost Waste:** Strategic discovery that wasting a single high-value cake is more damaging than many low-cost snacks.

## 🛠️ Methodology
* **ABC Analysis:** Ranked 36 products by profit contribution.
* **CV Analysis:** Measured sales volatility to separate stable sellers from erratic event-driven items.
* **Portfolio Matrix:** Categorized products into Stars, Opportunities, Workhorses, and Dogs.

## 🚀 Key Insights
* **The 80/20 Rule:** 47% of products generate over 80% of total profit.
* **Core Drivers:** Chocolate and Butterscotch cakes contribute nearly 23% of shop profit.
* **Predictability:** Items like **Momos** and **Sandwiches** are highly stable (CV < 20%).

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
1. **Target Opportunities:** Increase marketing for high-profit items like Pizzas to turn them into "Stars".
2. **Pre-Order Model:** Encourage pre-orders for expensive cakes to eliminate the ₹400+ per-unit waste loss.
3. **Safety Stock:** Implement a safety stock approach for volatile items like birthday supplies.
